Patron Piece: Hand-Stitched Mushroom Hat

One-of-One Wearable Artifact

Earlier in my life, I became seriously ill. Surviving that illness gave me a second chance, and it changed the direction of my life. After that experience, I realized that what I really wanted to become was a kind of healer.

Later, when I spent time at Stanford, I would often look out from the hill and see the streets below filled with people experiencing homelessness. It created a strange contrast. Up on the hill were some of the most advanced institutions in the world, full of knowledge and resources. But looking out at the city, it sometimes felt empty -- because the people who needed help the most were still outside that system.

That's when something became very clear to me. The reason I survived my illness and got a second chance was simple: I had access to medicine. Many others don't.

That realization motivated me to invent Frame -- a technology designed to help discover new medicines and make treatments more accessible, especially for people struggling with mental illness.

But the way medicine is funded today creates challenges. Many of the compounds that could help people most -- including psychedelic compounds used in mental health treatment -- are off-patent. Because they cannot be easily owned or monopolized, they are often difficult to develop within traditional venture-capital systems that depend on exclusivity and predictable profits.

So I'm trying a different path.

Instead of relying entirely on traditional investors, I'm inviting people to support the work directly.

The mushroom hat you see here is part of that effort.

I created it after running a workshop at Burning Man inspired by mushrooms -- symbols of life, renewal, and hidden intelligence. Mushrooms are also the source of powerful medicines found in nature. Compounds like psilocybin have shown real potential to help people heal from deep mental illness.

This hat is a hand-stitched, one-of-one wearable artifact, made entirely from upcycled materials. Every piece I make carries its own history through the materials used, the marks of the maker's hand, and the time embedded in the stitching. Because of that, no two pieces can ever be replicated.

The mushroom itself represents growth through unseen networks, regeneration, and transformation. Just as mycelium spreads quietly beneath the surface before anything appears above ground, new forms of healing and discovery often begin invisibly before they change the world.

This object is offered as a patron piece -- a way to support ongoing work at the intersection of art, technology, and human well-being.

Your support helps me continue developing tools that teach software how to design new molecules the way an artist learns to draw -- exploring new possibilities for medicines that could help people who currently cannot access the treatments they need.

Ownership of this piece is less about a transaction and more about stewardship -- supporting a vision where art, science, and healing grow together outside traditional market pressures.

If this story resonates with you, I invite you to support the work and help make new forms of medicine accessible to the people who need them most.
