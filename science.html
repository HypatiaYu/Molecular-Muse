Chemoenzymatic Synthesis of Aminooxy Sialyl Lewis X

Homogeneously glycosylated proteins are critical for both basic scientific investigations and the development of therapeutic biologics. Creating such proteins efficiently remains a significant challenge. One promising strategy is to use non-natural protein–glycan linkages that closely mimic native structures, which can streamline the production of glycoengineered proteins.

In this work, we present a method that enables site-specific and homogeneous glycosylation by chemically attaching aminooxy-functionalized glycans to recombinant proteins via stable oxime bonds. This approach leverages the aldehyde tag technique to introduce a formylglycine residue at a defined site within the protein. Using a newly developed synthetic route, we prepared complex aminooxy glycans through N-pentenoyl hydroxamate intermediates, which are amenable to both chemical and enzymatic modifications.

Demonstrating that this method is compatible with aminooxy-sialyl Lewis X is particularly important because SLex is a biologically significant and structurally complex glycan epitope involved in cell adhesion and immune regulation. Successfully attaching this glycan confirms the method's ability to handle functionally relevant and synthetically challenging glycans, underscoring its value for both research and therapeutic applications.
